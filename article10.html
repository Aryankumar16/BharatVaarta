The Development of Art in Mughal Era

The Mughal Empire was a very influential period of India ruled between the 16th and 18th centuries, which saw an exponential increase in artistic and cultural developments. It was also a time when Indian art flourished to great extents through the patronage by Mughal emperors, which brought both Persian and Indian traditions under one style, creating that unmatched beauty.

Mughal Painting

The most significant achievement of the Mughal period was the development of Mughal paintings. The style evolved through a combination of Persian miniatures painting and Indian style painting traditions. Mughal painters were masters of naturalistic images of court activities, hunting expeditions and historical events. The color used, minute details, and careful observation of the perspective represented Mughal paintings.

Mughal architecture is another magnificent legacy of this era. Inspired by Persian and Islamic architecture, the Mughal architects produced magnificent monuments that blended both Islamic and Indian architectural styles. Perhaps the most iconic example of Mughal architecture is the Taj Mahal, a magnificent mausoleum built by Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Other notable examples include the Red Fort in Delhi, the Jama Masjid, and the Buland Darwaza.

Mughal Calligraphy

Calligraphy was another art form that took on great significance during the Mughal period. Persian calligraphy, with its flowing and elegant script, was used throughout the manuscripts, inscriptions, and architectural decorations of the Mughals. Mughal calligraphers have their own style and blend Indian elements in Persian script.

Mughal Literature

The Mughal period also witnessed the start of a great literary past. Persian was the court language and saw many Persian poets and historians bloom under the wings of Mughal patronage. However, besides Persian, the Mughal emperors promoted the growth of regional languages, Hindi, and Urdu. The legacy of poets like Amir Khusro and of the Delhi Sultanate is still alive in the literature of the Mughals.

Decline of Mughal Art:

Decline in the 18th century of the Mughal Empire also resulted in decline in the production of artworks. However, the influence of Mughal emperors continued on the Indian art and culture as well. The art, architecture, and calligraphy styles left by Mughal emperors leave a rich legacy for India. This fusion of Persian and Indian traditions enriches the Indian cultural heritage. The richness of Mughal art inspires many Indian artists and craftsmen up to date.
