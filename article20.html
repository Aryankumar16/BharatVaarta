The arrival of the Europeans.

The advent of the European powers had come to alter the future course of India in an altogether new form. European powers, starting with Portugal, the Dutch, and then the French, felt the siren song calling them to spice and new trade routes within the subcontinent. In the entire chain of this process were involved events, which could be said to have radically changed the very course of destiny for that country.

The Early Portuguese Venturers
The first Europeans to enter India were the Portuguese. In 1498, the famous Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama succeeded in crossing the Cape of Good Hope and landed on the Malabar Coast. This voyage opened the first direct sea route to India, bypassing the traditional land routes controlled by the Ottoman Empire. Soon after, the Portuguese set up trading posts along the western coast of India, especially in Goa, which they captured in 1510.

The rule was characterized by cruel money-grabbing measures complemented by military muscle in the hands of Portuguese overlords. Heavy imposition was done on Indians that comprised paying heavy taxes plus monopolistic pocket for colonial spice trades; administration of such was founded on these pillars.By the end of the 17th century, the Portuguese was facing a dwindling entity status as other emergent forces from Europe appeared.The Dutch Challengers

The Dutch East India Company, or VOC, was founded in 1602 and came to India with an emphasis on trade and commerce. They set up trading posts along the western coast but then became more focused on the eastern coast, especially the Coromandel Coast. The Dutch were expert merchants and also efficient administrators. They created a strong naval fleet and competed intensely with the Portuguese for dominance of the spice trade.

The Dutch established Pulicat as the center, which is near to Madras. They gained other centers of trade very important in nature such as Masulipatnam and Nagapatnam as well. Their expansion though was not inclusive since its base was essentially on commericial aspects rather on territorial ones.

The Ambitions of French
The French East India Company was founded in 1664. The French came relatively late to India, as compared to the Portuguese and Dutch. They started trading posts in Surat and Pondicherry initially. The French, under the leadership of Dupleix, had a dream of a territorial empire in India. They exploited the crumbling Mughal Empire and the rivalry between the Indian rulers to extend their hold.
The French engaged a series of wars with British East India Company known as the Carnatic Wars, which they won and expanded much territory, but through better military and financial forces of the British, thwarted their ultimate goal of huge empire building.

The European Powers Legacy
The arrival of the Portuguese, Dutch, and French affects India severely. New ideas and influence in diverse cultures were brought about. Though the colonizers lead the traditional industries of the nation to decline and result in exploitation, they helped India grab its chance to be represented worldwide in commerce and trade because of high economic growth, which resulted in easier culture exchange between nations.
European powers also shaped India's political and social change. Their interference in the local conflicts and establishment of colonial administrations paved the way for the eventual British subjugation of the Indian subcontinent.
