The Delhi Sultanate and its Cultural legacy

The Delhi Sultanate was an Islamic empire, which dominated most of the Indian subcontinent between the 13th and the 16th century. This has left a permanent imprint on the region's cultural, social, and political sphere. This was really an interesting period as it represented an interesting synthesis of Islamic and Indian traditions to shape the Indian subcontinent. It all began with the Turks.

The Delhi Sultanate was established by the Turkic Mamluk dynasty in 1206 CE. The Mamluks, who were originally slaves and soldiers, seized power after defeating the last Hindu kingdom of Delhi. Their rule ushered in a succession of Turkic and Afghan dynasties that would rule India for the next three centuries.

Cultural Exchange and Synthesis

The most important features of the Delhi Sultanate were the cultural exchange and synthesis that occurred between the Islamic and Indian traditions. Many of them formed patrons of art and culture, encouraging the development of different forms of art: architecture, literature, and music.

Architecture: In the Delhi Sultanate period, many magnificent mosques, tombs, and forts were constructed. Qutub Minar was a towering minaret, and Jama Masjid, one of the largest mosques in India, was also the most iconic of the Sultanate architectural expressions. This group showed characteristics of both Islamic and Hindu styles, thus establishing the unique Indo-Islamic architectural tradition. Literature: The court language of the Delhi Sultanate was Persian. It soon spread throughout the Indian subcontinent. Not only that, but even the Sultans began to patronize the regional languages such as Hindi and Urdu. There was this new genre of Indo-Persian literature which came with the amalgamation of the Persian poetic forms and themes and sensibilities of the Indians. Music: The Delhi Sultanate also had their influence on Indian music. The Persian and Indian music traditions merged and created new kinds of classical music, for example, Hindustani classical music.

Legacy of the Delhi Sultanate

This is very much the case of Delhi Sultanate, though not without turbulent history and various dynastic changes, gave a legacy to the subcontinent of India. Merging Islamic and Indian influences that happened during this phase continues to shape the modern cultural landscape of the place. Architectural monuments that were produced during this epoch, literary traditions, or musical forms continue to get celebrated and admired.

Delhi Sultanate also played an immense role in developing the culture of Indo-Islamic art. The period is that of synthesis of Islamic traditions with Indian traditions, therefore the foundation stone was set on which the Mughal Empire would rise later on.
