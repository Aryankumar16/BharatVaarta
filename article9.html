The Bhakti Movement

The Bhakti Movement: A Spiritual Renaissance

This was an epochal phenomenon, both spiritual and social in nature, that developed in India during the middle ages. This movement is seen as a reaction toward the rigid caste system followed in Hinduism as well as the complicated rituals. The Bhaktas, or the Bhakti saints, wanted a much more direct and personal affair with God, stressing on love, devotion, and surrendering themselves to God.

Important Features of Bhakti Movement:

Personal Devotion: The Bhakti saints laid much emphasis on personal devotion to God. They opposed the strict caste system and insisted on the equality of all in the eyes of God.
Emotional Expression: The Bhakti poets had expressed love for God by writing passionate and emotive poetry. They used simple language and imagery to voice their feelings, making it accessible to people from different walks of life.
God as Beloved: God was always represented as a beloved, a lover, or a friend. This personal and intimate relationship is the core of the Bhakti movement.
Sankirtan : Sankirtan is that form of devotional singing and dancing wherein Bhakti saints often used to indulge. This type of congregational worship helped create the sense of community and shared spiritual experience.

Prominent Bhakti Saints

Ramanuja: He is considered a saint in Vaishnavism, and codified necessity for bhakti- devotion to Vishnu-became his most clear-cut contribution. He brought forth the thought of Vishishtadvaita, whereby the monistic and dualistic realities of life are made congruent with each other.
Basavanna: This 12th-century philosopher and social reformer condemned the stringency of caste system by stressing that all beings came into the world equal in birth. Basavanna's Lingayatism movement emphasized the need to adore Shiva as a linga.
Kabir: This was a mystic poet of the 15th century who rejects all the ritualistic and dogmatic views of Hindus as well as Islam. He stresses upon the fact that all religion is identical through the internal, spiritual experiences.
Mirabai: A princess, woman mystic from the 16th century and poet; Mirabai was ardent for Krishna's love. His passion love songs for Krishna made many generations be devoted.

Influence of the Bhakti Movement

The Bhakti movement was a crucial force for Indian society and culture. It challenged the rigid social hierarchy and promoted religious tolerance, inspiring social and religious reforms. It was only through the emphasis of the Bhakti saints on personal devotion and emotional expression that various devotional traditions could be developed in India.
